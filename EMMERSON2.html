LEARNINGS OF INTRO TO COMPUTING

Chapter 1: Introduction to Computing

In this section, I explored the historical aspects of computing, revisiting important milestones and innovations that influenced the development of the field. Additionally, I identified the inventors and their groundbreaking creations.

Chapter 2: Professions & Careers in Computing

This chapter opened my eyes to the diverse landscape of IT professions and their crucial roles in driving the computing industry forward. I explored the various specializations within IT, each requiring unique skillsets and expertise. This newfound understanding has equipped me with a clearer vision of the skills and knowledge I need to cultivate in order to thrive as an IT professional.

Chapters 3 and 4: Information Processing Cycle

I learned how information goes through steps: inputting it, processing or doing something with it, storing it, and then getting it out when needed. It's like a journey for data, helping us understand how computers work.

Chapter 5: Basic Concepts of Computer Security

This part talked about important ideas like keeping information secret, making sure it's correct, and always being able to use it when needed. It also looked into problems with staying safe on computers, like on the main system, when connected to others, and when using databases.

Chapter 6: The Internet & World Wide Web

Exploring the story of the Internet, I found out about its parts and how rules, services, and ways of doing things help different computer stuff work together. I got the basics of how the Internet is set up and how addresses work. Also, I checked out how the World Wide Web, the programs we use to explore it, and the big computers that store web pages all fit into the picture.

Chapter 7: Basic HTML & CSS

This section on Basic HTML & CSS was really interesting and a bit challenging for me. Learning how to build web pages with HTML and style them with CSS was fun and challenging. It was like balancing art and coding, which made it even more interesting. Overall, it was a cool experience that made me feel accomplished and excited about what I can do in web development.
